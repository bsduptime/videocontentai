# Chapter 2: Hook Diagnosis

Evaluate the first 3-15 seconds of a finished clip. The reviewer doesn't suggest what the
hook *should* be — they diagnose whether the existing hook works and why/why not.

---

## 2.1 The Decision Window

| Platform | Decision window | Benchmark |
|----------|----------------|-----------|
| YouTube Shorts / TikTok / Reels | 1-3 seconds | 65%+ hold at 3s = strong |
| YouTube long-form | 5-10 seconds | 70%+ hold at 30s = strong |

55% of long-form viewers drop within the first 60 seconds. The hook is the highest-leverage
moment in the entire video.

---

## 2.2 Hook Diagnostic Checklist

Score each criterion. Total out of 100.

| Criterion | Points | What to Look For |
|-----------|--------|-----------------|
| **Immediate visual engagement** | 0-15 | First frame: is it visually interesting? Face present? Motion? Color? Or is it a black frame / static shot? |
| **Value promise within 5s** | 0-20 | Can the viewer articulate what this video will give them after 5 seconds? If not, the hook fails. |
| **Curiosity gap created** | 0-20 | Is there an unanswered question, teased outcome, or unresolved tension? Or does the opening tell everything? |
| **No wasted frames** | 0-15 | No branded intro >3s before the hook. No "hey guys." No dead air. No logos. |
| **Stakes established by 15s** | 0-15 | Does the viewer understand what's at risk, what they'll learn, or why they should care? |
| **Audio energy appropriate** | 0-15 | Is the delivery energetic enough? Is music present and supportive? Is the pace 2.5-3.5 words/sec? |

### Grading

| Score | Verdict | Action |
|-------|---------|--------|
| 80-100 | Strong hook | No action needed |
| 60-79 | Acceptable | Note weaknesses, no hold |
| 40-59 | Weak hook | ISSUE — recommend rework |
| <40 | Failed hook | BLOCKER — will not retain viewers |

---

## 2.3 Hook Template Classification

Classify the hook against known templates to assess fit:

| Template | Signal Words / Patterns | Best For |
|----------|------------------------|----------|
| **Question** | "?", "Have you ever", "What if", "Why does" | Educational, long-form |
| **Stat/Number** | Numbers + surprise context ("only", "actually") | LinkedIn, data-driven |
| **Story (cold open)** | Past tense, "So there I was", sensory details | Personal brand, founder |
| **Demonstration** | Minimal speech, visual action, before/after | Tutorials, screen recordings |
| **Transformation** | "From X to Y", "went from", time-bound results | Case studies, benchmarks |
| **Contrarian** | "Everyone thinks", "Stop doing", "X is wrong" | Hot takes, thought leadership |
| **Urgency/FOMO** | "Before it's too late", "right now", "just discovered" | News, trends |

A hook matching **no template** is a warning sign — it likely lacks a proven psychological
trigger. A hook matching **multiple templates** is strong.

---

## 2.4 Hook vs. Cut Plan Alignment

Compare the finished hook to what was planned:

| Check | What to Compare |
|-------|----------------|
| **Segment match** | Does the hook use the segments the cut plan selected? |
| **Duration match** | Is the hook within the spec's duration range? |
| **Template match** | Does the hook match the editorial_lens from the cut spec? |
| **Title/description coherence** | Does the hook deliver on the title/description the plan generated? |
| **Thumbnail moment** | Is there a thumbnail-worthy frame in the first 30 seconds? |

Misalignment between plan and execution is an ISSUE — it means either the plan was wrong
or the execution drifted.

---

## 2.5 First-Frame Analysis

The very first frame determines scroll-or-stay on short-form platforms.

| Quality | What It Means |
|---------|--------------|
| **Face present** | +30% CTR (eye-tracking: faces dominate gaze) |
| **Expression visible** | Emotion (surprise, excitement) increases engagement |
| **High contrast** | Readable at mobile thumbnail size |
| **Text overlay** | Essential for short-form (60%+ watch muted) |
| **Motion in first 0.5s** | Captures peripheral attention via dorsal pathway |
| **Black frame** | BLOCKER — immediate scroll-away |

---

## 2.6 Sound-Off Test

For short-form content: mute the audio and watch the first 5 seconds.

- Can you understand the hook from visuals + text alone?
- Is there text overlay that conveys the value promise?
- Does the visual tell a story without audio?

If the answer to all three is "no," the hook fails the 60%+ muted-viewer test. This is an
ISSUE for short-form content, a NOTE for long-form.
