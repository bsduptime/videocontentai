## 1. MrBeast Editing Methodology (Leaked Production Document)

Source: [MrBeast leaked 36-page production guide](https://simonwillison.net/2024/Sep/15/how-to-succeed-in-mrbeast-production/), [ProTunesOne analysis](https://protunesone.com/blog/leaked-mrbeast-document-on-his-youtube-strategies/), [Tubefilter](https://www.tubefilter.com/2024/03/04/mrbeast-editing-style-number-of-cuts-per-video/)

### Video Segment Structure (the "MrBeast Blueprint")

| Segment | Goal | Technique |
|---------|------|-----------|
| **First minute** | Match thumbnail promise, prove the video delivers | Front-load visuals, music, effects, quick scene changes. Maximum information density. This is where the most viewers leave. |
| **Minutes 1-3** | Hook emotionally via "crazy progression" | Cover multiple days/events rapidly instead of dwelling on the first moment. If video is about surviving in a forest, cover several days, not just day 1. |
| **~3 min mark** | Re-engagement moment | Insert something "highly interesting and impressive" — a spectacle only you can execute. Prevents mid-video drop-off. |
| **Minutes 3-6** | Build story investment | "Most exciting and interesting content that is also very simple." Lots of quick scene changes. Make viewer fall in love with the story and people. |
| **Halfway point** | Second re-engagement | Justify remaining viewing time — explain what's still coming. |
| **Back half to end** | Maintain energy, never signal the end | Keep quality high. Avoid any cues that the video is winding down. |

### Cut Frequency Evolution

- **Old style (peak "retention editing"):** 38 cuts in 60 seconds (~1.6s per cut)
- **New style (2024+):** 23 cuts in 60 seconds (~2.6s per cut)
- **General cadence:** Cuts every 3-7 seconds with surprise twists and curiosity gaps
- MrBeast himself stated: slowing down, focusing on storytelling, adding breathers, cutting screaming = views skyrocketed

### Three Success Metrics

- **CTR** (Click-Through Rate): thumbnail + title + idea
- **AVD** (Average View Duration): how long viewers watch
- **AVP** (Average View Percentage): what fraction of total video is watched

### Production Rules

- Thumbnail and title created FIRST; all content designed to match
- Never spend >$10K on something not visible on camera
- The "wow factor": create something distinctive competitors cannot replicate
- Never repeat the same format consecutively

## 2. Cut Frequency Benchmarks by Content Type

Sources: [AIR Media-Tech](https://air.io/en/youtube-hacks/advanced-retention-editing-cutting-patterns-that-keep-viewers-past-minute-8), [YTShark](https://ytshark.com/youtube-video-editing-tips/), [VidPros](https://vidpros.com/video-clip-length/)

### By Content Format

| Content Type | Cut Interval | Notes |
|-------------|-------------|-------|
| **Talking head (young audience 13-24)** | 15-25 seconds | Visual change (angle, zoom, B-roll, cutaway) |
| **Talking head (older audience 25+)** | 20-40 seconds | Cut only when topic/tone clearly shifts |
| **Screen recording / tutorial** | 10-20 seconds per logical segment | Shorter clips for simple actions, longer for multi-step |
| **Software demo** | Balance detail and pace | Short clips for simple actions, longer for processes |
| **Online lectures / explainers** | 10-20 second clips | Segment topics logically |
| **Action/energetic content** | 3-7 seconds | Quick cuts, high stimulation |
| **Short-form (Reels/Shorts)** | 0.5-1 second cuts | 3.3x higher completion rates on mobile |

### Progressive Cut Widening (AIR Media-Tech)

Start tight, widen as the audience commits:

- **Intro:** Visual resets every 10-20 seconds
- **Once hooked:** Widen to 25-40 seconds between cuts
- **Educational content alternation:** Fast explanation (micro cuts every 10-15s) alternating with slow focus (holds up to 40s for visuals/examples)

### By Video Segment (Pacing Arc)

| Segment | Pacing | Technique |
|---------|--------|-----------|
| **Minutes 0-3** | High energy, frequent visual changes | B-roll or visual shifts every 10-15s |
| **Minutes 3-7** | Stabilize, fewer cuts | More contextual B-roll, wider shots |
| **After minute 8** | Mixed: calm + energy bursts | Reaction inserts, data pop-ups, emotional beats |

## 3. J-Cuts and L-Cuts

Sources: [Artlist](https://artlist.io/blog/j-cut-vs-l-cut/), [TechSmith](https://www.techsmith.com/blog/how-to-edit-videos-l-cuts-and-j-cuts/), [Soundstripe](https://www.soundstripe.com/blogs/a-video-editors-guide-to-j-cuts-and-l-cuts)

### Definitions

- **J-Cut:** Audio from the NEXT clip starts BEFORE the video cuts. Audio leads picture. Creates anticipation — viewer subconsciously anticipates what's coming.
- **L-Cut:** Audio from the CURRENT clip continues AFTER the video has cut to the next shot. Lets reactions land, provides continuity.

### When to Use (YouTube Context)

| Technique | Best For | Effect |
|-----------|----------|--------|
| **J-Cut** | Leading into new speaker, sound, or scene | Sparks curiosity, smoother pacing, keeps attention |
| **L-Cut** | Staying on listener reaction, letting music/sound continue | Emotional weight, reaction shots, breathing room |
| **Both** | Any transition between segments | Makes dialogue and transitions feel natural and emotionally resonant |

### Implementation Rules

- J-cut audio lead: 0.5-1.5 seconds of next clip's audio before video cuts
- L-cut audio trail: 0.5-2.0 seconds of previous clip's audio after video cuts
- Both prevent the "hard cut" feel that makes viewers conscious of editing
- Especially effective in B-roll transitions and cinematic YouTube content

## 4. Jump Cut Cadence

Sources: [Adobe](https://www.adobe.com/creativecloud/video/post-production/cuts-in-film/jump-cut.html), [VEGAS](https://www.vegascreativesoftware.com/us/video-editing/what-is-jump-cut-editing-and-how-to-use-it/), [Awesome Video Makers](https://awesomevideomakers.com/jump-cuts/)

### When Jump Cuts Work

- Removing dead space, tangents, repeated thoughts, filler words
- Tightening pacing in talking-head content
- Creating energy and momentum
- Signaling casual/authentic tone (vlog style)

### When Jump Cuts Feel Jarring

- Cutting every few seconds with no visual variety (one vlogger: longest spell without a cut was 8 seconds in a 6-minute video = described as jarring)
- Jump cuts without purpose (hiding awkward pauses rather than tightening content)
- No matching actions or sound design to smooth the transition

### Rules for Good Jump Cuts

- **Purpose test:** A good jump cut removes something unnecessary. A bad one just hides a pause.
- **Visual break cadence:** Every 20-30 seconds of talking head needs a visual break (screen recording, stock clip, product close-up, text animation)
- **Smoothing techniques:** Match actions or sound effects between shots; adjust timing to scene rhythm
- **Energy cues:** Factor in music energy and lighting changes to mask cuts
- **Diegetic smoothing:** Use ambient sounds, camera movement, or directional motion to ease transitions

## 5. Pattern Interrupt Timing

Sources: [2Bridges Productions](https://www.2bridges.nyc/nycblog/how-to-spice-up-videos-with-pattern-interrupts), [AIR Media-Tech](https://air.io/en/youtube-hacks/advanced-retention-editing-cutting-patterns-that-keep-viewers-past-minute-8), [LightningIM](https://lightningim.com/digital-tools/12-powerful-pattern-interrupt-video-editing-techniques-that-boost-engagement/), [Diana Briceno](https://www.dianabriceno.com/pattern-interrupt-for-viewer-retention/)

### Core Timing Rules

| Rule | Timing | Detail |
|------|--------|--------|
| **Maximum static duration** | 15 seconds | If you go longer than 15s without a visual/audio shift, expect drop-off |
| **Camera angle changes** | Every 15-20 seconds | Good starting frequency for talking head |
| **Visual change interval** | Every 15-25 seconds | Angle, B-roll, zoom, or cutaway |
| **First pattern interrupt** | 25-35 second mark | Change in camera angle, music drop, or sound effect |
| **Attention full reset** | Every 1-2 minutes | Short pacing shift or fresh visual |
| **Burst-to-calm cycle** | Every 2-3 minutes | 5-10 quick cuts clustered, then return to calm pacing |

### Opening Section (First 60 Seconds)

- B-roll or visual shifts every 10-15 seconds
- Cycle through: talking head -> screen share -> close-up -> quick cutaway
- Signals momentum and reassures new viewers the video won't drag

### Types of Pattern Interrupts

1. **Camera angle change** (physical or simulated zoom)
2. **B-roll cutaway** (supplemental footage)
3. **Text overlay / graphic** (highlight key point)
4. **Sound effect / music shift** (audio reset)
5. **Zoom in/out** (Ken Burns or punch-in)
6. **Speed change** (time lapse, slow motion)
7. **Color grade shift** (flashback, emphasis)
8. **Split screen / picture-in-picture**

### Impact Data

- Pattern interrupts can increase watch time by up to 85%
- Retention jumped 15% in one week when visual pacing was improved (cooking channel case study)

## 6. Walter Murch — "In the Blink of an Eye"

Sources: [StudioBinder](https://www.studiobinder.com/blog/walter-murch-rule-of-six/), [No Film School](https://nofilmschool.com/2016/11/6-rules-good-cutting-according-oscar-winning-editor-walter-murch), [Go Into The Story](https://gointothestory.blcklst.com/walter-murch-and-the-rule-of-six-47de34be834a), [Mike Battle book notes](https://www.mikebattle.com/book-notes/in-the-blink-of-an-eye)

### The Rule of Six (Priority-Weighted)

| Priority | Criterion | Weight | Description |
|----------|-----------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | **Emotion** | 51% | Is the cut true to the emotion of the moment? |
| 2 | **Story** | 23% | Does it advance the story? |
| 3 | **Rhythm** | 10% | Is it rhythmically interesting and "right"? |
| 4 | **Eye-trace** | 7% | Does it respect where the audience is looking? |
| 5 | **2D plane (planarity)** | 5% | Does it respect the grammar of 3D -> 2D? |
| 6 | **3D spatial continuity** | 4% | Does it respect physical space relationships? |

**Key insight:** Emotion (51%) outweighs ALL other criteria combined (49%). If the cut connects emotionally, small breaks in eye-trace or technical rules are acceptable.

**Hierarchy rule:** Never sacrifice a higher-priority criterion for a lower one. Emotion > Story > Rhythm > Eye-trace > Planarity > Spatial continuity.

### The Blink Theory

- Humans blink at thought transition points, not for eye lubrication
- Blinks coincide with "rhythmic digestion of thoughts and ideas"
- Murch discovered that where actors blink is often the ideal cut point (just before the blink)
- Editors make ~24 decisions per second: "No. No. No. No. Yes!"

### Cuts Per Minute Benchmarks (from the book)

| Scene Type | Cuts/Minute | Seconds/Cut |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Action sequence** | ~25 | ~2.4s |
| **Dialogue scene** | ~6 | ~10s |

These rates statistically align with natural human blink rates in equivalent emotional states.

### Core Principle

"Always try to do the most with the least." Avoid cutting for cutting's sake.

### Adaptation for YouTube/Digital

- **Emotion still comes first:** Even in tutorials, the viewer's emotional state (confusion, curiosity, satisfaction) should drive cut decisions
- **Rhythm maps to pacing:** The "music of the cut" directly parallels pattern interrupt timing
- **Eye-trace maps to zoom targets:** Where the viewer is looking on screen = where to place emphasis effects
- **Story advancement = information progression:** Each cut should deliver new value

## 7. Paddy Galloway's Retention Framework

Sources: [Creator Science podcast #152](https://podcast.creatorscience.com/paddy-galloway/), [Creator Science podcast #209](https://podcast.creatorscience.com/paddy-galloway-2/), [SigmaStory](https://sigmastory.in/paddy-galloway-the-youtube-mastermind-behind-mrbeast-viral-success/)

### Retention Danger Zones

Galloway examines retention curves to identify consistent drop-off patterns. His approach:

1. **Analyze retention curves** across multiple videos on a channel
2. **Identify recurring danger zones** where viewers consistently leave
3. **Diagnose root causes** (pacing, content, structure)
4. **Pre-produce solutions** — write 3-4 different intro versions, record all, pick the best

### Curiosity Loop / Open Loop Technique

- Create an unanswered question in the thumbnail/title that the video must resolve
- Example: thumbnail with "waste of money" next to a laptop = viewer must watch to learn why
- Entertainment channels: tension + open loop in thumbnail
- Educational/tech review: fewer than 4 words + curiosity loop

### Pre-Production Focus

Galloway puts most of his focus on pre-production — the phase most creators skip. Planning the video structure before shooting is where retention is actually won or lost.

## 8. Ali Abdaal's HIVE Framework

Sources: [Ali Abdaal Twitter/X thread](https://x.com/AliAbdaal/status/1499083172843302913), [Ali Abdaal ultimate YouTube guide](https://aliabdaal.com/youtube/the-ultimate-guide-to-youtube/)

### HIVE Structure

| Phase | Timing | Rule |
|-------|--------|------|
| **Hook** | First 10 seconds | Spend 10-20 minutes thinking about ONLY these 10 seconds before filming. Use: enticing B-roll, question, transformation, fact, or quick tip. |
| **Intro** | After hook | Set the tone, transition from hook to main content. Three video types: Discoverable (attract new), Community (deepen existing), Sales (drive action). |
| **Value** | Body | Heart of the video. Use practical examples at different stages to provide resolution to daily problems. Trim all filler — stick to core message. |
| **End Screen** | Last 10 seconds | Always relate to another correlating video. Set up the problem viewers still have, then point to the next video. |

### Editing Signatures

- **Text overlays** highlighting important points (trademark technique)
- **Smooth transitions** between sections
- **Practical examples** woven through to maintain engagement
- Retention rates of 70-80% on end screens when properly setting up the next video's problem

## 11. YouTube Retention Curve Research

Sources: [OpusClip](https://www.opus.pro/blog/youtube-retention-graphs-explained), [NarrationBox](https://narrationbox.com/blog/why-viewers-drop-off-after-30-seconds-youtube), [SocialRails](https://socialrails.com/blog/youtube-audience-retention-complete-guide)

### Retention Curve Patterns

| Pattern | Meaning | Action |
|---------|---------|--------|
| **Sharp early drop** | Weak hook or thumbnail/content mismatch | Rewrite intro, eliminate branded intros >3s |
| **Steady decline** | Pacing fatigue, no pattern interrupts | Add visual/narrative resets every 1-2 minutes |
| **Localized sharp drops** | Content bottleneck | Identify: jargon, repetition, or intrusive ad break |
| **Upward spikes** | High-value or rewatchable moment | Replicate these moments; they indicate what viewers value most |
| **Flat sections** | Strong engagement | Analyze what's working in these sections |

### Critical Thresholds

| Metric | Benchmark | Interpretation |
|--------|-----------|----------------|
| **First 30 seconds** | Hold 70%+ of viewers | Strong position for algorithm pickup |
| **First 10-15 seconds** | Below 50% = hook failure | Immediate rewrite needed |
| **First 30 seconds** | Losing >40% of viewers | Intro needs work |
| **Average video** | Only 24% of viewers remain by end | Normal; aggressive editing is critical |
| **First minute** | >50% drop-off is common | Most critical editing zone |
| **8-minute mark** | Loyalty compounds here | YouTube rewards with higher suggested placement + mid-roll ads available |

### Hook Structure (First 30 Seconds)

| Timestamp | Function |
|-----------|----------|
| 0:00-0:05 | Attention grab (shock, tease, question, or clip) |
| 0:05-0:15 | Clarify promise (what this video will deliver) |
| 0:15-0:30 | Establish stakes, context, or start journey |

### What Kills Retention (Data-Backed)

1. Hook-title mismatch (promise unfulfilled in first 15-30s)
2. Robotic/flat delivery (no pitch variation, uniform pacing)
3. Cognitive overload (too much context frontloaded)
4. Unclear value proposition (viewer needs clear outcome within 20s)
5. Greetings longer than 3 seconds
6. Flashy transitions (often hurt retention, contrary to intuition)
7. Linear pacing without resets (no pattern interrupts)

## 12. Hayden Hillier-Smith (MrBeast's Editor)

Sources: [Musicbed interview](https://www.musicbed.com/articles/filmmaking/masterminding-viral-edits-with-hayden-hillier-smith/), [Edit Like an Artist course](https://hayden-hillier-smith.mykajabi.com/edit-like-an-artist)

### Core Philosophy: Storyshowing vs. Storytelling

"If you can make me feel the story rather than tell me the story, that's compelling."

- Minimize voiceover; generate emotion/feeling visually
- Ask: "Can I show this instead of narrating it?"

### The "2+2" Principle (from Andrew Stanton)

Give the audience partial information and let them complete the puzzle. Audiences want to work for understanding without realizing it. Don't spell everything out — give 2+2 and let them arrive at 4.

### Music as Foundation (Not Supplement)

Workflow:
1. Draft content
2. Identify desired emotional tones
3. Create multiple varied playlists matching feelings
4. Test different musical styles before committing

Music fundamentally transforms tone and pacing. Example: period drama music transformed a Pokemon card purchase into an exclusive event.

### Anti-Retention-Hacking Stance

- "There is no such thing as retention hacking"
- Learn WHY to edit, not just how to hack metrics
- Don't "over-edit" for retention — make videos that "stir hearts and minds"
- Intentionally hold back editing in serious moments: no music, slow cuts, no jump cuts = realism that makes those moments more impactful

### Proactive vs. Reactive Editing

- **Reactive:** Tight deadlines force you to react to footage (compromised)
- **Proactive:** Tell the footage what to become (superior, requires time)

## Summary: Implementable Rules for AI Video Editing

### Universal Rules (All Content Types)

1. Never go more than 15 seconds without some visual or audio change
2. First 30 seconds must hold 70%+ of viewers or the hook has failed
3. Emotion is the #1 priority for every cut decision (Murch: 51%)
4. Every cut should remove something unnecessary or add something valuable
5. Pattern interrupt every 1-2 minutes (full attention reset)
6. Eliminate greetings over 3 seconds
7. Deliver value before introduction

### By Content Type

**Talking Head:**
- Visual change every 15-25s (young) or 20-40s (older audience)
- Jump cuts to remove filler, with B-roll breaks every 20-30s
- J-cuts to lead into new topics (audio 0.5-1.5s ahead of video)

**Screen Recording / Tutorial:**
- Cut every 10-20s aligned to logical steps
- Micro-cuts every 10-15s during fast explanation
- Hold up to 40s during detailed visual demonstrations
- Zoom into small UI elements at relevant moments

**Demo / Product:**
- Balance detail and pace
- Short clips for simple actions, longer for multi-step
- Text overlays for key stats/features

### Pacing Arc (for videos 8+ minutes)

| Phase | Minutes | Energy | Cut Interval |
|-------|---------|--------|-------------|
| Hook | 0:00-0:30 | Maximum | 10-15s visual shifts |
| Establish | 0:30-3:00 | High | 10-20s, "crazy progression" |
| Re-engage #1 | ~3:00 | Spike | Spectacle moment |
| Build | 3:00-6:00 | Medium-high | 15-25s, story investment |
| Stabilize | 6:00-8:00 | Medium | 20-40s, contextual B-roll |
| Re-engage #2 | ~halfway | Spike | Justify remaining time |
| Sustain | 8:00+ | Mixed bursts | Calm + energy alternation every 2-3 min |
| End | Final 10s | Redirect | Point to next video |
